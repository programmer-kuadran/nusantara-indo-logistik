Definition of Exports and Their Purpose and Benefits for the Economy of a Country

Posted on 01 November 2020 by Admin
  • 20 View
  • 12 comments
  • 50 share

Most of us have learned about exports and imports when we were in school. In short, Export is the activity of selling goods abroad. Meanwhile, imports are buying goods from abroad.

In this article we will briefly review what Exporters, Exporters, and what are the purposes and benefits for the economy in a country.

Definition of Export

Export is an activity of removing goods from the customs area. What is a customs area? This customs area is a part of the territory of the Republic of Indonesia which includes the land area, water territory and air space above it, also includes certain places in the Exclusive Economic Zone and the continental shelf.

In simple terms, export is defined as the activity of releasing goods from within the country to abroad in compliance with applicable provisions and regulations.

Export activities are usually carried out by a country if the country produces goods in large quantities and the need for these goods has been met in the country so that the production of these goods is sent to a country that cannot produce the goods or because the amount of goods produced in the destination country is not fulfilled .

What is an Exporter?

An exporter is an individual or legal entity that exports. If the export activity is on a large scale, then the shipment will involve Customs and Excise who is tasked with controlling the traffic of goods within a country.

Every item to be exported has its own terms depending on the type of goods. Not everyone can export because there are procedures that must be followed. Basically, the export procedure is easier than the import procedure, where the import procedure has more rules in it, especially regarding the payment of taxes. While exports are not, several types of products or goods are subject to tax, including exports of wood, rattan, and crude palm oil (CPO).

Destination and Benefits of Export

The following are the objectives and benefits obtained from exporting goods from within to overseas:

1. Growing Domestic Industry

Export is a form of international trade activity that aims to stimulate domestic demand which has led to the growth of large factory industries. An increasing demand for the export of a product can have an impact on the industrial development of a country. This of course can create a conducive business climate. In addition, by conducting international trade a country can get used to competing in the international market and be trained in fierce competition.

2. Control Product Prices

By exporting, the country aims to take advantage of the excess installed capacity of a product. The aim is to control the prices of export products that are in the country. When a product is abundantly produced, the price of that product in the country will have a low price because it is very easy to obtain. Therefore, to control prices so that they remain stable, countries export to other countries that need these products.

3. Adding Foreign Exchange

Export activities certainly have a positive impact on a country's economic growth. The benefits of exporting activities are opening up new markets abroad as an expansion of the domestic market, growing investment, and increasing a country's foreign exchange.

That was the definition of Export, Exporters, and their purpose and benefits in the economy of a country. In the next article we will discuss the meaning of imports, importers, and their purposes and benefits in the economy of a country.
