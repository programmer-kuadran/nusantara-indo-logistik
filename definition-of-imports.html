Definition of Imports and Their Purpose and Benefits for the Economy of a Country

Posted on 01 November 2020 by Admin
  • 20 View
  • 12 comments
  • 50 share
  • life style, friends, wonderful

In the previous article, we discussed the meaning of Exports, Exporters, and their goals and benefits for the economy of a country. Well, in this article we will discuss the opposite activity, namely Import.

Definition of Import

Import is an activity to enter goods into the Customs Area or also an activity of purchasing goods or services from other countries to meet domestic needs. For example, Indonesia does not have a wheat commodity so that to meet the supply and demand for domestic wheat, it is necessary to bring wheat from wheat producing countries to Indonesia. As with export activities, large-scale shipping activities of imported goods require assistance from customs. Usually, the government will increase the tax rate on imported products to importers.

This causes imported goods to have a higher price because the price has already been subject to taxes which are then borne by the consumers. So, therefore don't be surprised if imported goods tend to be more expensive when compared to the price of local products.

Not just any goods that can be allowed to enter as imported goods. The Customs and Excise Directorate has clear regulations on what goods are allowed and prohibited for import activities. Individuals or legal entities that carry out imports are referred to as Importers. Not just anyone can import products or goods from abroad. Compared to export procedures, imports are more regulated and more difficult to process, especially in terms of taxation.

Purpose and Benefits of Imports

The main objective of import activities is to meet domestic needs. Export and import activities are a form of inter-connectivity in each country. No country is capable of living independently.

In meeting its needs, there must at least be one thing that makes this country have to build good relations with other countries. One of them, in this economic activity. Another objective of import activities is to strengthen the balance of payments and reduce foreign exchange flows out of the country.

Then what are the benefits of imports? The benefit of the import activity itself is that it allows a country to obtain raw materials, goods and services for a product that is limited in number domestically or that cannot be produced domestically. This indirectly supports the stability of the country.

Examples of Products Imported by Indonesia

The following are some examples of products that are mostly imported into Indonesia, including:

1. Raw Materials

In the raw material category, Indonesia's largest imported commodities are helicopter equipment and mechanical aircraft engines, electronic equipment, iron and steel. The supply of aircraft and helicopter equipment is still imported from other countries because PT Dirgantara Indonesia itself is still unable to produce its own helicopters and planes.

2. Food Sector

Meanwhile, in the food sector, Indonesia's largest imported commodities are frozen animal meat, fruits, rice and soybeans. Surprisingly, even though Indonesia has many rice fields, it turns out that the need for rice in Indonesia is still insufficient, so it needs imports from other countries, such as Vietnam, Thailand and India.

That was our discussion regarding the Definition of Import and Its Purpose and Benefits for the Economy of a Country. Of course we hope that Indonesia can meet the food needs of its people without the need for a lot of imports. Because supposedly with such a large agricultural land, Indonesia should be the largest Food Exporter in the world.
